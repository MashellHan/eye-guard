# EyeGuard Product Spec v0.1

> Medical-grade eye health guardian for macOS.

## Product Vision

EyeGuard is a native macOS menu bar application that monitors screen time and reminds users to rest their eyes based on medical evidence. It implements the **AAO (American Academy of Ophthalmology) 20-20-20 rule** and **WHO screen time guidelines** to protect users' eye health through smart activity detection, tiered notification escalation, and daily health scoring.

**Core principle**: Prevention over treatment. Small, consistent breaks dramatically reduce digital eye strain (computer vision syndrome).

**Target users**: Knowledge workers, developers, designers, students - anyone spending 4+ hours daily on a Mac.

## v0.1 MVP Features

### 1. Menu Bar Presence

- Persistent menu bar icon (eye symbol)
- Session timer showing current continuous usage
- Click to open popover with quick stats
- Start/pause session control

### 2. Activity Monitoring

- **Keyboard monitoring** via `CGEventTap` (key down events)
- **Mouse monitoring** via `CGEventTap` (mouse move, click events)
- **Idle detection**: 30-second inactivity threshold
  - After 30s of no keyboard/mouse activity, timer pauses automatically
  - Timer resumes immediately on next input event
- **Session tracking**: Continuous use time, total active time, break count
- Privacy: No keystrokes or content logged. Only event timestamps for activity detection

### 3. 20-20-20 Micro-Break

- **Trigger**: Every 20 minutes of continuous screen use
- **Action**: Remind user to look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
- **Medical basis**: AAO 20-20-20 rule - reduces accommodative stress and blink rate recovery
- **Behavior**: Timer countdown in notification. Auto-dismisses after 20 seconds
- **Snooze**: User can snooze once for 5 minutes

### 4. Hourly Macro-Break

- **Trigger**: Every 60 minutes of continuous screen use
- **Action**: Suggest 5-10 minute break - stand up, stretch, walk
- **Medical basis**: WHO recommends hourly breaks for VDT (video display terminal) workers
- **Behavior**: Persistent notification until acknowledged or break taken
- **Detection**: If idle for 5+ minutes within window, counts as break taken

### 5. Mandatory Break Alert

- **Trigger**: 120 minutes of continuous use without a qualifying break (5+ min)
- **Action**: Full-screen overlay with countdown and health warning
- **Medical basis**: Extended continuous use causes severe accommodative fatigue
- **Behavior**: Cannot be easily dismissed. Requires 5-minute break minimum
- **Escalation**: This is always Tier 3 notification level

### 6. macOS Notification Center Integration

- Uses `UNUserNotificationCenter` for Tier 1 alerts
- Requests notification permission on first launch
- Supports notification actions (Start Break, Snooze, Dismiss)
- Respects system Do Not Disturb settings

### 7. Basic Preferences

- **Micro-break interval**: 15 / 20 / 25 / 30 minutes (default: 20)
- **Macro-break interval**: 45 / 60 / 90 minutes (default: 60)
- **Mandatory break threshold**: 90 / 120 / 150 minutes (default: 120)
- **Idle detection threshold**: 15 / 30 / 60 seconds (default: 30)
- **Enable/disable** each break type independently
- **Launch at login** toggle
- **Sound effects** toggle

## Notification Escalation System (3 Tiers)

### Tier 1: Standard Notification Banner

- **Style**: Native macOS notification via Notification Center
- **Content**: Eye emoji + friendly message + countdown
- **Example**: "Time for a micro-break! Look 20 feet away for 20 seconds."
- **Behavior**: Auto-dismisses after 20 seconds for micro-breaks. Persists for macro-breaks
- **Interaction**: Action buttons - "Start Break", "Snooze 5 min"

### Tier 2: Floating Translucent Overlay

- **Trigger**: After 2 consecutive ignored Tier 1 notifications
- **Style**: Floating translucent card, top-right corner of screen
- **Size**: ~300x200 pt, always-on-top
- **Content**: Countdown timer, eye health tip, break suggestion
- **Opacity**: 85% background blur
- **Behavior**: Stays visible until break taken or dismissed
- **Dismissal**: Click "I'll take a break" or detected idle period

### Tier 3: Full-Screen Semi-Transparent Overlay

- **Trigger**: 120+ minutes continuous use OR 3+ consecutive ignored notifications
- **Style**: Full-screen overlay with blurred background
- **Opacity**: 70% dark overlay with centered content card
- **Content**: Large countdown timer, health warning message, eye health score impact
- **Message**: "You've been using your screen for [X] hours continuously. Your eyes need rest now."
- **Behavior**: Requires minimum 5-minute break. Cannot be quickly dismissed
- **Exit**: Only after countdown completes OR user clicks "Emergency Override" (logged, affects score)

## Health Score System (0-100)

Daily health score calculated from 4 weighted components:

### Break Compliance (40 points)

| Metric | Scoring |
|--------|---------|
| Micro-break compliance rate | 0-20 pts (linear: 100% compliance = 20 pts) |
| Macro-break compliance rate | 0-15 pts (linear: 100% compliance = 15 pts) |
| No mandatory break triggers | 5 pts (binary: 0 if triggered, 5 if not) |

### Continuous Use Discipline (30 points)

| Metric | Scoring |
|--------|---------|
| Longest continuous session < 60 min | 15 pts (linear decay from 15 to 0 as session exceeds 60 min) |
| Average continuous session < 30 min | 10 pts (linear: avg 20 min = 10 pts, avg 40 min = 5 pts) |
| No sessions > 120 min | 5 pts (binary) |

### Total Screen Time (20 points)

| Daily Screen Time | Points |
|-------------------|--------|
| < 4 hours | 20 |
| 4-6 hours | 15 |
| 6-8 hours | 10 |
| 8-10 hours | 5 |
| > 10 hours | 0 |

### Break Quality (10 points)

| Metric | Scoring |
|--------|---------|
| Average break duration meets target | 0-5 pts |
| Breaks include actual idle time (not just dismissed) | 0-5 pts |

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Rating | Color |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 90-100 | Excellent | Green |
| 75-89 | Good | Blue |
| 60-74 | Fair | Yellow |
| 40-59 | Poor | Orange |
| 0-39 | Critical | Red |

## Technical Requirements

- **Platform**: macOS 14.0+ (Sonoma and later)
- **Language**: Swift 5.9+
- **UI Framework**: SwiftUI + AppKit (for menu bar and overlays)
- **Architecture**: MVVM with service layer
- **Permissions**: Accessibility (for CGEventTap), Notifications
- **Storage**: UserDefaults for preferences, file system for reports
- **Memory target**: < 30 MB resident
- **CPU target**: < 1% average, < 5% during event processing
